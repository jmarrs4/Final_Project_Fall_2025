    The Big Apple

    New York is no stranger to frigid winters and mediocre sports teams. Yet when we look into the data they actually have a negative correlation between crime and the temperature. However, this doesn't hold true for all types of weather. Looking at our box plot and our uv index chart you can see that other "Warm Weather" staples like the sun and clearl skies bring out more crime in the city.

    Crime Severity in NYC

    When separated into violent and non violent crimes New York City has a positive correlation between violent crimes and higher temperatures, but a negative correlation between higher temperatures and nonviolent crimes. New York is known for their winters (even though they aren't as bad as Chicago's) and it clearly slows down violent criminals. However, it seems that crimes are still being committed, they are simply nonviolent.

    Conclusions

    New York City is an interesting case in data. When you just look at the temperature it appears that more crimes are actually committed in the cold. However, when you look at different weather factors there does appear to be a correlation between colder weather and crime. Additionally, the fact that their is a strong positive correlation between violent crime and warm weather. However, since our first graph just looked at temperature the NYC data appears to disprove the original hypothesis.
